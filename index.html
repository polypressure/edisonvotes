by voting in the Edison LSC and PTO elections
on Wednesday, April 13

Why Is This so Crucial?

Many of you have asked, is signing the Petition going to do any good or have any impact? More recently, you've also asked, does the petition still have any legs, and is there anything we can still do to help keep Mrs. Oberhardt as principal of Edison Regional Gifted Center?

Well, now you have the opportunity to turn your petition signature into something that is concrete, actionable, and helps to achieve the goal of keeping Mrs. Oberhardt as our principal: by voting in the LSC and PTO elections for candidates that support Mrs. Oberhardt.

Next

What to Look for in Candidates

We recommend you vote for candidates who:

  • Believe Mrs. Oberhardt is still the most qualified person to serve as Edison's principal—especially when compared to the replacement candidates proposed by the Principal Selection Committee.
  • Like most of you, have signed the petition, give continued support to Mrs. Oberhardt, and support her appeal.
  • Are committed to helping the Edison community heal, by reflecting carefully on what was done poorly by the previous LSC, correcting their missteps— and ensuring that when the Edison community is ready to move forward, it doesn't end up in the same place.
  • Are enthusiastic about making the LSC and PTO truly transparent, accessible, inclusive, and accountable.
  • Are sincerely dedicated to building consensus and alignment across families with diverse backgrounds and needs—rather than dividing and polarizing the community.
  • Are committed to working positively with the school leadership—whether it's Mrs Oberhardt or a new principal—to help Edison navigate the CPS budget crisis and maintain its current level of educational excellence.

Mrs. Oberhardt's Appeal

Mrs Oberhardt’s appeal process is still very much alive, and the LSC election on April 13th will play a crucial role in determining her future at Edison.

If Mrs Oberhardt wins her appeal, she will likely return to Edison on a short-term contract, perhaps as short as six months, after which she will face another LSC renewal vote.

The canidates chosen during this election to serve in the LSC will decide whether or not to renew her contract if she does win her appeal.

Ernesto Pérez

Father of Sebastian, a second grader

Hello, my name is Ernesto Pérez and, along with my wife, Maria, we are the parents of Sebastian, a second grader here at Edison. I have lived in Chicago for most of my life and have always appreciated the diversity among the people who live here, not only in terms of ethnicity but also in the wide range of social, economic and cultural differences. I believe that when a person experiences a wider range of beliefs and practices, especially those that challenge one’s own, that it not only benefits that individual, but the community as a whole.

Professionally, my background is in art, design and technology, having worked in various fields including Editorial Design, Web Consulting, Video Game Development and Teaching at the university level. During these experiences I have been in both leadership and supporting roles, working with groups that were physically in the same spaces as well as contributors who were located elsewhere, sometimes on the other side of the World. As a result of my past experiences I am a proponent of art, both visual and performance, as part of a well-rounded education and embrace technology not just as a tool for learning but also as a way to enable better communication.

As a member of the LSC I hope to promote transparency in not just the process of improving the school but also within the community itself. I welcome all opinions, suggestions and views and will work to find ways to best address the concerns of the school as a whole while trying to create an environment of open discussion. Technology can be harnessed to bring people together and, in the case of Edison, I will strive to use it to allow those who may not have the time to fully engage in the community to have more of a voice. It is obvious that the last couple of months have been challenging to the school community and I hope to not only help find solutions to the issues at hand, but also assist in developing an atmosphere where grievances can be aired and dealt with in a healthy manner.

Looking forward to serving our community, I thank you in advanced for your support.

Jim Magas

Father of Stella, a third grader

My name is Jim Magas. My daughter Stella is currently enrolled as a 3rd-grader at Edison. I am a Michigan native and Chicago resident since 1995. Our family resides in the Portage Park neighborhood. I’ve been involved in many facets of the music industry for over 20 years, primarily in retail. I’ve been involved in community-building, large event planning and global marketing.

I am humbly asking for the opportunity to represent you as a member of the Edison Regional Gifted Center Local School Council. Since our daughter Stella began attending kindergarten in 2012, we’ve felt a strong sense of gratitude and honor to attend such an esteemed school. It is really a dream come true. We love the convivial atmosphere, dedicated staff, accomplished student body and visionary administration. Serving on the Local School Council is a way to give back to the school that has provided so much for us.

It’s no secret that our school has seen its share of recent controversy. There will be disagreements and challenges ahead, but we all share the common goal of wanting what is best for our children and our school. Our diverse and intelligent community is made up of many voices and they all need to be heard. Through respectful discussion and healthy debate, we can all become wiser as we move forward.

As an elected member of the Local School Council, I will respectfully listen to everyone. The LSC serves to represent all parents. It will be my responsibility, as your representative, to ensure that the LSC is transparent and accountable to the student body, parents, faculty and community. I am familiar with the challenges that working families face to be more involved and I will seek new ways to engage the community.

It will be my pleasure to serve you. Thank you for your consideration.

Will Scanlan

Father of Keelan, a first grader

Hello everyone, this William Scanlan, 1st grade parent. I am running for treasurer of the PTO. As owner of a custom cabinetry business called Will Products Inc that has been in business since 1988, I am solely responsible for financial oversight, banking responsibilities, reimbursements check writing functions, taxes, state filings, licenses, billing and payroll. I incorporated this business in 1991 myself and have been doing the S-Corp taxes for 25 years. Previously I made a living as a self employed musician (Duchamp, Rearrangement and See Saw) and cabinetmaker. I have been a home owner since 2003 with rental income property for several years. I have been doing my own taxes for 40 years. My skills can be of benefit to the PTO.

I will advocate for the fee to join the PTO to be “pay what you can” – I would like as many of the parents, staff and teachers to be members of the PTO as possible. I also hope to encourage more “Teachers” in the Parents and Teachers Organization.

I will advocate for eliminating the competition between grades during Track-a-Thon. Some grades have families more able to contribute than others. I do not feel we should compete with each other. I believe we can strive for a common goal instead to which everyone can contribute according to their means.

I will advocate for being able to vote in the PTO elections the same day one becomes a member regardless of when that is. Currently one must be a member for thirty days in order to vote. I see no reason for this.

I will suggest that we send annual statements of contributions to our members for tax purposes along with statements showing where the money goes.

Our objectives as the PTO are to promote the education of the students of Thomas A. Edison Regional Gifted Center and to foster a spirit of cooperation between parents, faculty, and students. Feel free to contact me with any suggestions or comments – willproducts@aol.com – Thank You!

Ben Whitford

Father of Elena, a first grader

I'm the father of a first-grader, and I'll work to represent the views and needs of families in Edison's lower grades. My daughter has a condition requiring a 504 plan, so I'm committed to advocating for kids with special needs. And we’re a Spanish-speaking family, so I'll make sure the LSC's decisions reflect the full diversity of Edison’s student body.

As a reporter — for the Guardian, for Newsweek, and recently as US correspondent for the Ecologist — I've covered a wide range of educational and local-government issues, including a stint on a team investigating disciplinary issues in New York public schools, and several months reporting on gangs in a Bronx high school. I've spent my career holding people and organizations accountable, and I'll use my experience to ensure the LSC does its job well, and to keep Edison on track under its new leadership.

I've also spent time working in classrooms: during my college years I served as a classroom aide and ESL teacher in schools in the UK, Spain and Hong Kong, and spent a year as a special-ed assistant for a 7-year-old boy with autism. I’ve seen many different school systems, both functional and dysfunctional, and my breadth of experience will be a boon to the LSC.

As you'd expect from a journalist, I'm passionate about engagement and transparency, and I'm determined to make the LSC more proactive about communicating with and seeking feedback from parents and teachers. I'll make it a priority to keep Edison's very, very busy parents up to speed on LSC activities, and to find new ways to help everyone stay informed and involved.

Edison is on the brink of its biggest shakeup in years, and it's vital that we move beyond the tension surrounding Mrs Oberhardt's departure. If elected, I'll work to make the LSC an open, inclusive and positive space where all Edison families can come together and have a voice in the way the school is run. I firmly believe that we're all on the same team, and that it's by working together that we can best ensure a bright future — for Edison, and for all our children.

Jill Martensen

Community Representative Candidate, 8th grade parent

Sheila Quirke

Mother of Jay, a first grader

The day I learned my son, 1st grader Jay, was admitted to Edison, I had been watching out our front window to see when the mail truck would arrive. As soon as it pulled up, I ran out to see if that all important CPS letter had been delivered. It had, and as I read the words about my boy's acceptance to ERGC, I screamed in joy and jumped up and down in the middle of the street, hugging our mail carrier, and tried to explain my giddiness.

I hope to never lose that sense of joy and gratitude during our son's years at Edison.

Running for the LSC was not on my radar before this past winter. I was one of the many parents who felt blindsided by the non-renewal of Mrs. Oberhardt's contract. When it was suggested that feeling surprised or uninformed was a result of being disengaged, I vowed to correct that. I started attending meetings (my husband and I are the authors of the LSCrecaps you might have seen on the Facebook Friends of Edison page), met teachers and parents from other grades, and have gained a tremendous amount of respect for the process involved in local school governance.

My training is as a clinical social worker, and I spent over a decade working in the health care field with older adults. While my focus was always clinical, my skill set included facilitating groups, writing and presenting at conferences, advocating for vulnerable individuals and families within larger systems, and acting as a Field Instructor for the University of Chicago's School of Social Service Administration.

I left professional work in 2007 when our oldest child was diagnosed with cancer as a young toddler. After her death I returned to social work, but it didn't quite fit me in the same way. At that point I began blogging and using my growing platform to raise awareness for pediatric cancer. Very unexpectedly, a new career was born.

I now work as a freelance writer with a focus on parenting, grief, social justice, healthcare, and education. My work has been featured at ChicagoNow, mom.me, Huffington Post, sheknows, AOL, WGN, CBS2, Fox Business, and on NPR. Recently, I have also added local medical institutions to my client base, focusing on telling the story of healthcare through patient profiles. I maintain an active social media presence of 25K+ Facebook followers for my blog, which has educated me quite a bit on managing conflict and dynamic online debates. I feel quite proud that the little corner of the Internet that I manage has a reputation for being a thriving community that hosts open discussion on controversial topics while remaining respectful and sensitive in tone and content.

What I can contribute to the LSC is my enthusiasm, varied communication skills, social media savvy, and experience in creating consensus and working within larger systems. I have grown passionate about gifted education and protective of its need to be valued and funded appropriately.

I truly believe that it is possible to maintain confidentiality, something my social work background required extensive knowledge of, while also being transparent and engaging. Those things are not mutually exclusive. I hope to sit on an LSC that works to create bridges to the community and solicits a more active exchange with our dynamic parent community that has so very much to offer. You can help me do that with your vote.

Jason Adasiewicz

Father of Isabella, a first grader

Jason Adasiewicz has a daughter in 1st grade and a younger daughter that will be beginning full day preschool next year. Jason's greatest asset that he brings to the school is his work in the arts for the last 20 years. He is a vital member of the Chicago music scene and throughout the year plays over 100 concerts both nationally and internationally, some of which are benefits for nonprofits. His recordings have made many year end top 10 lists including the infamous NYT Pop and Jazz lists.

In addition to performing he has been one of a handful of folks that helped create what the Pitchfork Music Festival is today. His job for 10 years straight was to head the setup and teardown of the 18,000+ capacity outdoor festival; everything from climbing the big stages to troubleshooting how to deal with fields of mud created by sudden downpours to organizing perimeter control to keep hipster fence jumpers out.

The first thing on Jason's agenda would be to make sure we have an operating PA system, especially for the kid’s rock bands during the winter assembly! But a broader application of this would be to help organize public meetings and events so that the audience can focus on the activity, not on the logistics of making it run.

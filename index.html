Why Is This so Crucial?

Many of you have asked, is signing the Petition going to do any good or have any impact? More recently, you've also asked, does the petition still have any legs, and is there anything we can still do to help keep Mrs. Oberhardt as principal of Edison Regional Gifted Center?

Well, now you have the opportunity to turn your petition signature into something that is concrete, actionable, and helps to achieve the goal of keeping Mrs. Oberhardt as our principal: by voting in the LSC and PTO elections for candidates that support Mrs. Oberhardt.

Next

What to Look for in Candidates

We recommend you vote for candidates who:

  • Give continued support to Mrs. Oberhardt, as well as her appeal process.
  • Are committed to helping the Edison community heal, by reflecting carefully on what was done poorly by the previous LSC, correcting their missteps— and ensuring that when the Edison community is ready to move forward, it doesn't end up in the same place.
  • Are enthusiastic about making the LSC and PTO truly transparent, accessible, inclusive, and accountable.
  • Are sincerely dedicated to building consensus and alignment across families with diverse backgrounds and needs—rather than dividing and polarizing the community.

Mrs. Oberhardt's Appeal

Mrs Oberhardt’s appeal process is still very much alive, and the LSC election on April 13th will play a crucial role in determining her future at Edison.

If Mrs Oberhardt wins her appeal, she will likely return to Edison on a short-term contract, perhaps as short as six months, after which she will face another LSC renewal vote.

The canidates chosen during this election to serve in the LSC will decide whether or not to renew her contract if she does win her appeal.

Ernesto Pérez

Father of Sebastian, a second grader

Hello, my name is Ernesto Pérez and, along with my wife, Maria, we are the parents of Sebastian, a second grader here at Edison. I have lived in Chicago for most of my life and have always appreciated the diversity among the people who live here, not only in terms of ethnicity but also in the wide range of social, economic and cultural differences. I believe that when a person experiences a wider range of beliefs and practices, especially those that challenge one’s own, that it not only benefits that individual, but the community as a whole.

Professionally, my background is in art, design and technology, having worked in various fields including Editorial Design, Web Consulting, Video Game Development and Teaching at the university level. During these experiences I have been in both leadership and supporting roles, working with groups that were physically in the same spaces as well as contributors who were located elsewhere, sometimes on the other side of the World. As a result of my past experiences I am a proponent of art, both visual and performance, as part of a well-rounded education and embrace technology not just as a tool for learning but also as a way to enable better communication.

As a member of the LSC I hope to promote transparency in not just the process of improving the school but also within the community itself. I welcome all opinions, suggestions and views and will work to find ways to best address the concerns of the school as a whole while trying to create an environment of open discussion. Technology can be harnessed to bring people together and, in the case of Edison, I will strive to use it to allow those who may not have the time to fully engage in the community to have more of a voice. It is obvious that the last couple of months have been challenging to the school community and I hope to not only help find solutions to the issues at hand, but also assist in developing an atmosphere where grievances can be aired and dealt with in a healthy manner.

Looking forward to serving our community, I thank you in advanced for your support.

Sheila Quirke

Mother of Jay, a first grader

The day I learned my son, 1st grader Jay, was admitted to Edison, I had been watching out our front window to see when the mail truck would arrive. As soon as it pulled up, I ran out to see if that all important CPS letter had been delivered. It had, and as I read the words about my boy's acceptance to ERGC, I screamed in joy and jumped up and down in the middle of the street, hugging our mail carrier, and tried to explain my giddiness.

I hope to never lose that sense of joy and gratitude during our son's years at Edison.

Running for the LSC was not on my radar before this past winter. I was one of the many parents who felt blindsided by the non-renewal of Mrs. Oberhardt's contract. When it was suggested that feeling surprised or uninformed was a result of being disengaged, I vowed to correct that. I started attending meetings (my husband and I are the authors of the LSCrecaps you might have seen on the Facebook Friends of Edison page), met teachers and parents from other grades, and have gained a tremendous amount of respect for the process involved in local school governance.

My training is as a clinical social worker, and I spent over a decade working in the health care field with older adults. While my focus was always clinical, my skill set included facilitating groups, writing and presenting at conferences, advocating for vulnerable individuals and families within larger systems, and acting as a Field Instructor for the University of Chicago's School of Social Service Administration.

I left professional work in 2007 when our oldest child was diagnosed with cancer as a young toddler. After her death I returned to social work, but it didn't quite fit me in the same way. At that point I began blogging and using my growing platform to raise awareness for pediatric cancer. Very unexpectedly, a new career was born.

I now work as a freelance writer with a focus on parenting, grief, social justice, healthcare, and education. My work has been featured at ChicagoNow, mom.me, Huffington Post, sheknows, AOL, WGN, CBS2, Fox Business, and on NPR. Recently, I have also added local medical institutions to my client base, focusing on telling the story of healthcare through patient profiles. I maintain an active social media presence of 25K+ Facebook followers for my blog, which has educated me quite a bit on managing conflict and dynamic online debates. I feel quite proud that the little corner of the Internet that I manage has a reputation for being a thriving community that hosts open discussion on controversial topics while remaining respectful and sensitive in tone and content.

What I can contribute to the LSC is my enthusiasm, varied communication skills, social media savvy, and experience in creating consensus and working within larger systems. I have grown passionate about gifted education and protective of its need to be valued and funded appropriately.

I truly believe that it is possible to maintain confidentiality, something my social work background required extensive knowledge of, while also being transparent and engaging. Those things are not mutually exclusive. I hope to sit on an LSC that works to create bridges to the community and solicits a more active exchange with our dynamic parent community that has so very much to offer. You can help me do that with your vote.
